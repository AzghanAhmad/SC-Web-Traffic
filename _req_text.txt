ScribeCount Website TrafficScope of Work (SOW)ScribeCount Website Traffic & Behavior Intelligence ServiceFirst-Party Web Analytics, Conversions, Heatmaps, and Unified ReportingDocument Version: vNext (Architecture & Integration Focus)Client: ScribeCountService Name (Working): ScribeCount Web TrafficTarget Users: Independent authors and small author teamsPrimary Goal: Privacy-first website intelligence that merges cleanly with ScribeCount sales, email, and attribution data to produce actionable author insights1) Executive SummaryScribeCount will deliver a first-party Website Traffic and Behavior Intelligence service designed specifically for independent authors. The service will collect, normalize, and analyze traffic and engagement data from author-owned websites and storefronts, beginning with WordPress, Shopify, and Wix.This feature is not intended to replicate generic third-party analytics platforms. Instead, it is engineered to act as one of ScribeCount’s core signal sources, capturing reader intent and behavior on author websites and making that data usable across the ScribeCount ecosystem—particularly in reporting, attribution, email performance, and future recommendation systems.A defining requirement of this service is data sovereignty. All tracking is first-party, privacy-first, and processed exclusively within ScribeCount infrastructure. The output of the service is not raw analytics dashboards alone, but structured behavioral data that integrates with ScribeCount’s existing reporting model so authors can understand how marketing actions translate into outcomes.In practical terms, the Website Traffic feature enables authors—and ScribeCount—to answer questions that are currently fragmented across multiple tools:Which marketing channels send engaged readers, not just clicks?Which pages actually lead to signups, buy clicks, or purchases?Where do readers hesitate or drop off before converting?How do email campaigns, ads, and website behavior interact?What changed yesterday that deserves attention today?2) Objective (Expanded Narrative)2.1 What the Feature IsThe Website Traffic feature is a first-party analytics and behavior intelligence system tailored to how authors use websites as part of their publishing business.It includes:Website traffic measurement (sessions, visitors, pageviews, engagement)Acquisition analysis (source, medium, campaign, referrer)Content performance (landing pages, top pages, exit behavior where available)Conversion tracking (newsletter signups, buy clicks, purchases when supported)Funnel analysis (multi-step reader journeys)Heatmaps (click and scroll behavior)Daily summary snapshots for routine decision-makingAn internal data API that allows other ScribeCount systems to consume web behavior signals securelyThis feature treats the author’s website as a decision surface, not merely a content container. The goal is to make website data explain why other metrics inside ScribeCount move the way they do.2.2 How It Should Be ConstructedThe system must be designed with integration longevity as a primary constraint. As with ScribeCount Email, Website Traffic is a standalone operational service, but ScribeCount remains the unified reporting and intelligence hub.Construction principles include:Clear separation between collection, normalization, analytics, and reportingEvent-first design, where every meaningful user interaction produces a structured eventStrong schema discipline so data can join reliably with email, sales, and attribution datasetsVersioned internal contracts so downstream features can evolve safelyObservability and auditability suitable for support, compliance, and debuggingThe Website Traffic service should not “own” business conclusions. It owns behavioral facts. Interpretation and recommendations belong to ScribeCount’s reporting and intelligence layers.2.3 How Authors Will Use ItAuthors want clarity, not configuration overhead.From the author’s perspective, the experience should feel like:Connect site → confirm tracking worksSee meaningful data appear quicklyUnderstand what’s working and what isn’tMake better decisions without juggling toolsTypical author workflows supported by this feature include:Evaluating whether ads, social posts, or emails drive meaningful site engagementIdentifying which landing pages and book pages actually convertUnderstanding whether mobile readers behave differently than desktop readersImproving page layout and CTA placement using heatmapsMonitoring daily performance changes without logging into multiple systemsThe feature must support multi-site authors (main site, series sites, direct store domains) while presenting data in a way that remains comprehensible and actionable.3) How Website Traffic Data Integrates Into ScribeCountThe most important architectural requirement is that Website Traffic is not an isolated analytics silo. It is a shared behavioral data source designed to integrate cleanly with existing and future ScribeCount features.3.1 Integration with Email Marketing (Immediate)Website Traffic and ScribeCount Email are designed as complementary systems.Website data enables email reporting to answer higher-value questions:Did an email click lead to meaningful on-site engagement?Which email campaigns drive signups versus immediate buy clicks?Do certain segments behave differently once they reach the site?Conversely, email metadata (campaigns, flows, link groups) provides context that makes website traffic intelligible. This bidirectional relationship allows ScribeCount to report on email → site → outcome as a single narrative, rather than disconnected metrics.3.2 Integration with Sales and AttributionWebsite Traffic produces critical intermediary signals between marketing actions and sales outcomes:Landing page effectiveness before a purchaseBuy-click behavior even when purchases occur off-siteFunnel drop-offs that explain sales volatilityWhen combined with ScribeCount’s existing sales and retailer click-out data, this enables more accurate and more honest attribution—clearly distinguishing confirmed outcomes from directional signals.3.3 Integration with Daily Email and AlertsThe Website Traffic feature will generate a daily snapshot per site that feeds directly into the existing ScribeCount daily email.This snapshot is not a full report; it is a decision aid. It highlights:Core KPIs and their movementTop traffic sources and pagesConversion activityAnomalies that may warrant actionBy making website behavior part of the daily ScribeCount rhythm, the feature increases product engagement while reinforcing ScribeCount’s role as the author’s operational dashboard.3.4 Integration with Future Intelligence and RecommendationsWebsite Traffic lays the foundation for future ScribeCount capabilities, including:Automated optimization suggestions (page layout, CTA placement, funnel sequencing)Smarter campaign recommendations based on historical behavior patternsContent strategy insights (what topics or pages consistently convert)Cohort analysis tied to reader acquisition sourcesBecause the system exports normalized data through an internal API, these features can be added incrementally without re-architecting the traffic system itself.4) Target Architecture (Conceptual, Integration-First)4.1 Core ComponentsA) First-Party Collection LayerResponsible for capturing on-site behavior using a ScribeCount-controlled tracking mechanism. Enforces privacy, consent, and redaction rules at the edge.B) Ingestion & Normalization LayerValidates incoming events, applies enrichment (device, coarse geo), and produces normalized behavioral events aligned to ScribeCount’s shared event vocabulary.C) Analytics & Aggregation LayerOptimized for behavioral queries, rollups, and trend analysis. Produces query-ready datasets for dashboards, snapshots, and downstream services.D) Heatmap Aggregation LayerAggregates interaction data into privacy-safe heatmaps without session replay or keystroke capture.E) Snapshot & Reporting LayerProduces immutable daily summaries and supports fast dashboard rendering.F) Internal API & Export Layer (ScribeCount-Only)Provides secure, scoped access to web metrics and events for other ScribeCount systems. Enforces tenant isolation, versioning, and rate limits.4.2 Construction Guidance (No Code)Design for site-scoped isolation with tenant-level governanceNormalize early so downstream services never need provider-specific logicPrefer immutable reporting artifacts (snapshots) for reproducibilityTreat event schemas as contracts, not implementation detailsBuild connector modules as adapters, not special cases5) Benefits5.1 Benefits to AuthorsOne place to understand website performance and reader behaviorClear visibility into what marketing efforts actually convertPractical optimization tools (funnels, heatmaps) without invasive trackingReduced dependence on third-party analytics toolsDaily insight without analysis paralysis5.2 Benefits to ScribeCountA high-value behavioral signal that strengthens all reportingIncreased daily engagement and platform stickinessA foundation for premium analytics and recommendation featuresStrong privacy-first positioning in a post-third-party-cookie landscapeCleaner, more defensible attribution narratives6) Guiding Principles and ConstraintsFirst-party, privacy-first collection onlyNo fingerprinting or sensitive data captureNo outbound data exports beyond ScribeCountArchitecture-first documentation;
